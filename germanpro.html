GermanPro App

Beyond Duolingo, Past Babbel: Dive into True German Mastery!

The Challenge

German is often perceived as a difficult language to master due to its intricate grammar, noun genders, rich vocabulary, and complex syntax.

This perception can lead learners to feel 'stupid', demotivated, or frustrated, negatively impacting their language acquisition journey.

To address this, a German language app is in development, aiming to provide practical tools through personalized, context-rich learning experiences, along with interactive and immersive content.

The app's goal is to make learning German an intuitive, engaging, and adaptable process, tailored to the unique learning styles and paces of each user.

My Role

Sole UX Designer, in collaboration with German Teachers and Students.

Duration

January to Marzo 2023.

Tools

Understading the Users

A combination of research methods was applied, including interviews, surveys and contextual inquiries.

These are the main findings:

Quotes

"Can you Learn German solely through apps? No, absolutely not."
"No app can do that (yet)."
"Apps are not actual courses, they're just sort of fun practice games."
"It gamifys language learning to make it fun."

Behaviors

  • Users often start with language learning applications to build foundational skills before moving on to more comprehensive study methods. These methods may include formal coursework or a wider variety of learning resources, such as books, videos, or additional grammar applications.
  • Increasingly, learners are looking for language exchange partners to practice conversation.
  • Learners are in search of applications that provide a holistic approach, encompassing speaking, writing, and vocabulary development.
  • Some learners favor self-study and immersive experiences over structured classes. Immersive techniques might involve watching television in German or reading German texts.
  • Many learners also pursue supplementary resources to enhance their grasp of grammar and conversational skills beyond the capabilities of applications alone.
  • For vocabulary enhancement, a number of users commit to daily practice with applications as a targeted strategy.

Needs

  • A mix of learning tools, including both digital apps and traditional resources, to build a strong foundation in German.
  • A common goal is to supplement app-based learning with additional resources like grammar books, language exchanges, and immersion in media.
  • Immersive experiences and practical application opportunities, such as conversational practice with native speakers and exposure to German in necessary contexts.
  • Structured and comprehensive learning approaches for in-depth understanding, especially in grammar and syntax.
  • Personalized instruction, which could be through language exchange platforms or private tutoring, tailored to individual learning styles and needs.
  • Integration of learning with real-life use, aiming for immediate comprehension and usage without translation.
  • Access to a variety of materials that cater to different aspects of the language: speaking, listening, reading, and writing.
  • Resources that provide cultural context to enhance the learning experience and promote quicker assimilation of the language.

Frustrations

  • Frustrations regarding German Language Apps: The inadequacy of grammar explanations, lack of ample conversational practice in apps, a sense that progress within these apps doesn't equate to real-world fluency, and the repetitive, superficial nature of app-based language learning present significant drawbacks.
  • Grammar Complexity: Many learners find the German grammar system to be particularly daunting and hard to wrap.
  • Information Overload: The vast number of resources, methods, and strategies available for language learning can be overwhelming for some learners, making it hard to decide where to start or how to proceed.
  • Time Consumption: Learning German is a time-consuming process and can be overwhelming, especially for those who haven't learned a second language before or are busy.
  • Pace: Some individuals feel disheartened when they see others picking up the language faster than they do, leading to feelings of inadequacy or self-doubt.
  • Traditional Learning Methods: Textbook learning and classroom settings can be boring for some, reducing their motivation to study and learn.
  • Expectations: There can be a mismatch between the expectations of how quickly one should learn the language and the actual pace of progress.
  • Pressure to Achieve: Self-imposed pressure to reach a certain level of proficiency within a specific timeframe can make the learning process stressful.
  • Practice Opportunities: Without opportunities to practice speaking and listening in real-life situations, it can be difficult for learners to see practical progress.
  • Fear: A fear of speaking incorrectly and being judged by native speakers can hinder the willingness to practice speaking.

Facts

  • There is a shared understanding that while apps can be beneficial for initial learning, they are not sufficient for achieving advanced language proficiency on their own.
  • Language learning apps like Duolingo are recognized as valuable for building basic vocabulary and phrases, but are seen as only one part of a comprehensive study plan.
  • To reach beyond elementary proficiency levels, learners need more interactive and comprehensive methods, such as real-life conversation practice and formal instruction.
  • A combination of apps for vocabulary and grammar, along with practical application through tutoring or language exchanges, is often reported to be effective.
  • Fluency is typically not attained through app-based learning alone, and immersion in environments where language use is part of daily life is frequently recommended for advanced learning.
