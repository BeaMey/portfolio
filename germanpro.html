Conducting User Research

Understand users through interviews, surveys, observation, and other research methods.

Twenty-five people with a common interest in mastering the German language, of different ages (from 20 to 42 years old), and from various linguistic backgrounds, such as Spanish, Arabic, English, French, Latvian, Polish, Portuguese and Urdu, were surveyed.

This are the 10 main indings:

  • The main motivation for learning German is related to work (88%), followed by cultural integration (64%) and education (44%).
  • Most respondents are at the B2 proficiency level.
  • 48% have studied German for over a year.
  • Nearly half (48%) spend less than one hour per week studying German.
  • Grammar is a major challenge for 68%, with vocabulary and oral fluency being other key issues.
  • Strong preference for interactive learning content (72%), followed by text/audio (44%) and video (28%).
  • Progress is measured through conversations with native speakers (60%), but only 40% engage weekly, and 24% daily.
  • 60% value cultural context in learning German.
  • 64% use apps like Duolingo, DW, and Babbel.
  • Desired app features include interactive exercises (47.8%), grammar explanations (60.9%), and pronunciation practice (34.8%).

Four participants fluent in at least two languages were interviewed. All have experience using language apps:

  • Participant 1: 36-year-old in pharmaceuticals, speaks four languages.
  • Participant 2: 29-year-old in hospitality, fluent in two languages.
  • Participant 3: 31-year-old designer, fluent in three languages.
  • Participant 4: 30-year-old project manager, fluent in four languages.

Main Insights

  • Motivations include cultural exploration, personal connections, and career growth.
  • Methods include conversational practice, immersion, reading, and apps, alongside structured courses.
  • Popular apps are great for beginners but lack features for advanced learners like AI-driven conversational practice.
  • Apps complement traditional methods but are insufficient for real-world fluency.
