Research Goals

The very first step: ‚û°Ô∏è To deeply understand the problems faced by entrepreneurs when seeking legal advice.

The main objectives of the research were:

  1. Identify entrepreneurs' actions when they require legal assistance.
  2. Understand their most common legal needs.
  3. Uncover frustrations experienced in obtaining legal advice.
  4. Determine the optimal approach to meeting these needs and addressing frustrations.

Research Methods

A multifaceted approach was needed involving:

  • Competitive Analysis
  • Surveys and Interviews

Competitive Analysis

üìà The research project began with a competitive analysis to gather user insights and frustrations, identify product weaknesses, and uncover opportunities for differentiation and improvement.

üåé In Ecuador, there is currently a lack of highly developed legal apps, similar to those in the United States and Europe.

üì≤ To draw inspiration, we studied two popular platforms, LegalZoom and Rocket Lawyer, and incorporated feedback from app reviews, including App Review, TechCrunch, GetApp, and more.

Competitive Analysis Summary

Hypothesis Statement

    Based on the competitive analysis, the hypothesis is that incorporating these features will make the Untangled app not just useful and friendly but also desirable compared to its competitors:

    1. Network of Attorneys: Access to expert attorneys for personalized guidance.
    2. User-Friendly Platform: Intuitive design for easy navigation.
    3. Money-Back Guarantee.
    4. Wide range of legal services to meet different needs.
    5. Powerful Algorithm.
    6. Fast and Convenient Services: Swift access to legal services through an accessible app.
    7. Prompt Legal Assistance from attorneys when required.
    8. Intuitive Form Builder.
    9. Strong customer supportfor timely assistance.

Surveys and Interviews

Surveys provided preliminary insights, while interviews delved deeper into user preferences and requirements.

  • The interviews with the entrepreneurs covered a wide range of ages, from 28 to 54 years old, the age range in which most Ecuadorian entrepreneurs are found.
  • There were a total of 5Ô∏è‚É£ interviews.

Here's a quick summary of the 9 key findings:

  1. Competitive analysis-based hypothesis focuses on app features but neglects specific legal needs and regulatory awareness.
  2. People typically seek advice from their trusted network for basic legal matters but are open to using legal apps.
  3. Desired app features encompass FAQs, lawyer consultations, document templates, reminders, in-app payments, and real-time guidance.
  4. Interviewees prioritize payment transparency, flexible payment options, and good customer service.
  5. Common legal needs include model contracts, notary proceedings, affidavits, deeds, and intellectual property protection.
  6. Some individuals stay informed about legal changes through news sources and social media.
  7. For tax-related legal matters, nearly all users turn to their accountants.
  8. Full-time entrepreneurs require comprehensive legal support, especially in areas like tax, labor, and intellectual property protection.
  9. Most interviewees need a lawyer several times a year, particularly those involved in business operations.

The observations were analyzed, leading to the definition of central problems, referred to as "problem statement":

Problem Statement

Entrepreneurs often struggle to find reliable and affordable legal services to protect their interests and business in a timely and effective manner.

To address their needs, a web application was designed to provide them with cost-effective and convenient access to reliable legal advice and sources of information.

This app allows entrepreneurs to quickly resolve common legal inquiries, saving them time and resources in the process.

Card Sorting

Card sorting is a user research method where participants categorize information to improve website and app organization.

During the open card sorting exercise, where participants independently group and label website content, many important insights became evident. Here are the main ones:

  1. Many participants, unfamiliar with legal terms, struggled to classify items accurately. Despite this, maintaining the existing legal categorization made sense (Ask a Lawyer and DocCreator as 2 separate features).
  2. The exercise emphasized the importance of a comprehensive onboarding process to help users comprehend the application's functions and navigate it effectively.
  3. Also, to address user confusion, the feature Q&A section with FAQs should be added. Legal services can be complex for non-lawyers.
  4. The exercise highlighted the need to exclude "My legal documents" and "My lawyers' answers" from the "My profile" section and remove several cards to simplify processes.

Test: Validate Solutions

A usability test on Figma's medium-fidelity prototypes assessed user interaction ease. The feedback refined the prototype, leading to another test with the following specific parameters:

Tasks

Six users were assigned the following 4 tasks:

1. Making an appointment with a lawyer

2. Creating a non-disclosure agreement

3. Accessing all legal documents created

4. Accessing a lawyer's response

Research Objectives

1. Evaluate initial user interactions with the smartphone web app.

2. Determine app comprehension and perceived value.

3. Measure task completion and time.

4. Identify questions, needs, and errors during testing.

Target Audience

Entrepreneurs between 25 and 54 years old in Ecuador.

This age range represents 66.93% of entrepreneurs in Ecuador (source).

Methodology

1. Moderated in-person testing with two Ecuadorian and Berlin residents.

2. Moderated remote testing with four entrepreneurs in Quito, Ecuador.

Environment

The tests were administered both in-person and remotely.

Remote participants accessed the Figma prototype on their computers and shared their screens during the test, utilizing Microsoft Teams and Google Meetings according to the user's choice.

In-person testing took place at the user's home, and the sessions were recorded using a camera and tripod.

Metrics Collected

During the usability testing process, the following metrics were collected to assess the application's performance:

-Usability Problems: Assessed using the The Severity Ratings for Usability Problems Framework, that evaluates the severity of design issues.

-Level of Satisfaction: Gauged with the After-Scenario Questionnaire (ASQ) to collect feedback on participants' experience.

Discoverability Issues

The 6 usability tests revealed issues mostly related to discoverability. Users appreciated the app's utility but struggled with tasks 1, 3, and 4, and often confused between "profile" and "home".

Two Personas Emerged

User feedback has illuminated two distinct user categories:

  • Financially privileged entrepreneurs seeking elegance in the app
  • Lower-income users concerned about affordability and user-friendliness.

    • Based on the goal of this project, feedback from the target persona of entrepreneurs in need of affordable legal advice will be prioritized. However, this depends on business goals.

    The Need to Alleviate Legal Service Concerns

    During the usability test, I observed that certain users expressed apprehension regarding the cost of legal services and the complexity of legal issues, understandably so, as these topics can be unsettling for many. After conducting research on colors known to evoke a sense of calmness, I determined that using blue and green would be suitable color options for a legal app.

    A preference test regarding colors was performed. The results of the test revealed a significant distinction between blue and green colors, with 83% favoring blue. Therefore, blue tones were chosen. One user even remarked that blue is a classic, clean color often associated with trust.

    Visual Design

    To further convey a friendly and trustworthy image, this color palette was designed:

    To convey a friendly and trustworthy image, friendly and trustworthy visuals, such as approachable lawyer photos, were incorporated. These lawyer photographs are intended to effectively convey the seriousness and expertise of the lawyers while nurturing authenticity, trust, and empathy. The use of a white background further enhances a polished and professional aesthetic.

    To address confidentiality and image management concerns, I've replaced the pictures of the lawyers I previously collaborated with. As an illustration, I've included a photo of myself from my time practicing law, ensuring alignment with the app's parameters

    Language

    Finally, the app should maintain a friendly and approachable tone, ensuring that users feel understood and supported throughout their legal journey. By using non-judgmental language, the app aims to break down any barriers or stigma associated with legal issues. This approach fosters an environment of trust and encourages users to openly engage with their legal concerns, empowering them to seek the support they need confidently.

    Iteration: Last and Upcoming Steps

    Design Harmony and Accessibility Commitment

    High-fidelity wireframes were crafted using Gestalt Theory and Material Design Principles, focusing on clear organization, consistent elements, and a soothing palette of reassuring blue shades.

    The app is friendly to everyone! It follows guidelines to make sure the colors and text are easy to see. Nevertheless, incorporation of specialized technology to assist users with diverse abilities is pending.

    UX Evolution: Collaborative Refinement

    Untangled's design has been refined with the valuable input and suggestions from fellow UX designers, significantly shaping this latest version. The primary features are in place, but some issues still need attention. The "DocCreator" and Chatbot features, still need fine-tuning for optimal performance.

    User-Centered Assessment and Expansion

    Continued assessments are necessary to ensure alignment with business goals, including pricing accessibility.

    Furthermore, it's essential to assess user needs, preferences, and expectations with a specific focus on UI testing, A/B testing, accessibility and further usability testing.

    Lastly, I would carry out a separate analysis tailored to a different audience, such as lawyers, legal firms, or other service providers interested in offering this service.

    Final Reflections:

    Design Evolution:

    • The original and final versions of the web app have undergone significant changes. E.g. Initially, there was a lack of clarity regarding the home screen's purpose and the placement of the newsletter. However, by incorporating a newsletter on the home page featuring "latest relevant legal changes," users remained engaged. This addition may lead to increased interaction with the app and a more proactive approach to seeking legal assistance.
    • The need to introduce previously unconsidered services, such as a newsletter focused on entrepreneurs and the impact of regulatory changes, became evident, which had a broad impact on the overall design.
    • While the UI was improved through the use of tools like Figma and the implementation of Material Design principles and Gestalt Theory, there is still room for further enhancements.

    Market Differentiators:

    • There are similar legal apps internationally, but Untangled stands out for being tailored for Ecuadorian entrepreneurs.
    • Each country presents unique challenges due to their regulatory frameworks; our app recognizes and addresses these differences.
    • The design is unique, focusing on entrepreneurs seeking reliable legal services without the traditional high cost.
    • Untangled App has a section for regulatory updates, a particular need in contexts with frequent changes in the country.

    User Focus and Possible Improvements:

    • Addressing Lengthy Forms: The design should better tackle the issue of lengthy, tedious forms that discourage user engagement. One solution is to integrate AI, which can assist users in completing the legal document creation process more efficiently, ultimately increasing app usage.
    • App Personalization: There is an opportunity for further app personalization. Introducing a customization screen where users can input business-specific details would lead to highly specialized legal advice. This customization would also enable lawyers to access relevant information quickly, reducing the time spent gathering details.
    • Dual Demographic Targeting: While the primary goal is to assist entrepreneurs facing cost-related challenges in accessing legal services, there is potential in targeting affluent entrepreneurs who seek an elegant application. This demographic is often willing to pay for legal services. In the future, the app could cater to both demographics.
    • Conveying Trust and Tranquility: It's essential to convey trust and tranquility in the app design, especially considering that many users fear legal costs. The design should continue to prioritize creating a sense of trust and comfort for the tarjet users.
    • Collaborative Enrichment: Collaborating with legal firms and additional lawyers would have enriched the project, particularly by integrating a comprehensive list of feasible services and their corresponding prices. This collaborative approach would enhance the value and offerings of the app.

    Challenges and Tools:

    • Conducting usability tests alone was challenging; at times, I missed valuable details when noting user reactions. Moreover, not everyone was willing to be recorded, limiting subsequent review. Hence, I believe I should have conducted more tests, preferably in person, and with the support of an assistant.
    • My initial skills in Figma limited the design of the first interactive prototype, though over time I improved my proficiency with the tool.
    • Regarding UI, I would like to team up with a UI Designer to translate the findings on visual improvements.
    • Due to time constraints, collaborating with a legal firm or additional lawyers was no possible. It would indeed have enriched the project, particularly in terms of integrating a comprehensive list of feasible services and their corresponding prices.
