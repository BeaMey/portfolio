My Journey üõ§Ô∏è

Transitioning from Law to UX Design may seem unconventional, but it was fueled by my natural problem-solving abilities, curiosity, and innovation drive.

My legal background honed my analytical skills, research capabilities and dedication to service, but the frustration of legal outcomes tied to political influences or arbitral decisions pushed me to explore other business facets like legal marketing, content creation and other business development strategies. This journey not only revealed my aptitude for these areas but also my craving for creativity.

In User Experience Design (UX), I found my true calling, where empathy and problem-solving align. Follow my career change journey through this timeline, highlighting key turning points and milestones:


  • 2023

    UX Designer Vibes

    Fueled by curiosity, I have discovered my niche in UX design, where my love for research, problem solving, understanding users, creating connections, and designing IT solutions all come together.

  • 2022

    UX Dive and Marketing Moves

    After my first experience as a Content Creator outside of a law firm, I obtained a certification in UX Design. At the same time, I worked as a Marketing and Business Development Consultant for law firms in Latin America and Spain, taking them into the digital era.

  • 2021

    Exploring New Horizons

    In an inspection and auditing SaaS, I was able to combine content creation, analytics and my auditing experience, while soaking in the knowledge of developers and UX experts, which fueled my passion for UX. üí° Fun fact: Berlin became my career playground. Check out my work!

  • 2020

    Business Deep Dive

    Driven by the goal to transition from law to business development, I pursued an MBA focusing on innovation at ESMT Berlin. During this period, I gained recognition for my social media prowess and crafted a growth strategy for Leaf Labs.

  • 2018

    Lawyer Turned Storyteller

    During my tenure as an in-house lawyer at GlaxoSmithKline, I managed clients account, project development, and content marketing, revealing my aptitude for storytelling and problem-solving outside the legal realm.

  • 2017

    Shifting Legal Grounds

    I transitioned from human rights to corporate law, but bureaucracy and corruption prompted me to research and publish on intellectual property intersecting with human rights, while also pivoting to legal marketing and client management.

  • 2016

    Legal Beginnings

    I earned my law degree with honors, focusing on environmental law, and established myself as a human rights advocate, presenting cases before the Inter-American Court. Eventually, the weight of human rights work impacted my mental health. I found refuge in photography.
