A bit about me

From the highlands of Ecuador, 3,000 meters above sea level, to the vibrant streets of New York and Berlin's diverse tech scene, my career journey has been a rich tapestry of varied experiences and lessons. In Quito, my interest in social justice led me to explore the field of law, where I honed my research and writing skills. However, I soon realized the limitations of this path and shifted my focus to business development within the legal sector, finding a niche where my creativity could flourish.

My gap year in New York was transformative; it was there that I discovered a passion for photography, allowing me to fully immerse myself in creative pursuits. Berlin further shaped my professional trajectory, introducing me to the world of user-centered design. Each city and experience has been a pivotal chapter in my story, collectively influencing my growth and guiding me towards work that has real impact.

Transitioning from law to UX design was an unconventional choice, but it perfectly merged my love for creativity, research, and problem-solving. Now, my focus lies on projects that resonate with my values, particularly in environmental conservation, human rights, legal services, and education. I am dedicated to making a difference, striving for a career that balances my passions with meaningful impact.
